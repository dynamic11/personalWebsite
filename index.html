About Me

As a Master's student, I have focused my research on the topic of Computer-Aided Design for the simulation of electrical circuits. I enjoy my area of research as it has exposed me to the application of mathematics/numerical methods to efficiently simulate complex electrical circuits.

Outside of my area of research, I enjoy computer programming and web application design. I take every opportunity to learn new technologies and to develop practical tools. It is a rapidly changing area that requires continuous learning. As a developer, it is an exciting challenge to see a project grow from an idea to a fully functional application being used by real clients.

New Interests

In recent years, the terms Machine Learning and AI have been heavily advertised. Since my area of research is heavily math-based and I have had exposure to black-box modeling methods such as Vector Fitting, I quickly became curious to see what these keywords meant and to explore the underlying math. I took a course on the application of Machine Learning in the simulation of Electrical Circuits that gave me an academic introduction to the area. Since then, I have done some projects on my own time to try to learn more. Upon the completion of my Master's, I would like to focus my energy on this new area of technology.
